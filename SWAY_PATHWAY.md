## What's True Right Now

One node proven. One campus. One venue. Nine weeks.

- 255 check-ins. 155 users. 81% activation. 26% return rate.
- Zero incentives. Zero staff changes. Zero paid marketing.
- Pilot paused (students left for summer).
- No paying customers. No revenue.

That is the entire evidence base. Everything else is thesis.

## The Three Gates

Before Sway scales to anything — second campus, SaaS pricing, Wrapped, Circles, Community SaaS — three gates must be passed. In order. No skipping.

```
GATE 1 — The Graph Compounds
GATE 2 — The Operator Pays
GATE 3 — The Behavior Transfers
```

Each gate is a binary: pass or fail. Fail any one and the previous strategy is wrong.

---

## GATE 1 — The Graph Compounds
**Deadline: End of Fall 2026**

**Question:** Does the same identity show up across multiple nodes?

**What to prove:**
- A student checks into Gully's AND the gym AND Foundry AND one club — same identity, same semester
- At least 20–30 students build cross-venue histories
- The data starts to look like a graph, not an attendance log

**What to build for this gate:**
- NFC tap station at Gully's entrance (removes friction, makes the habit environmental)
- Endicott Gym onboarded as Node 2
- Foundry or Entrepreneurship Club onboarded as Node 3
- One off-campus Salem/Beverly venue as Node 4
- Identity persists across all four without re-registration

**How to measure:**
- Users with check-ins at 2+ nodes: target ≥25% of active users
- Users with check-ins at 3+ nodes: target ≥10%
- Average unique nodes per active user by week 9: target ≥1.8

**What failure looks like:**
- 90%+ of check-ins are Gully's-only
- Users who visit the gym don't scan
- Identity doesn't feel worth building outside a bar context

**If Gate 1 fails:** Sway is attendance software for campus bars. Rethink the model — either narrow to venue SaaS only (Blackbird model) or find a different behavior anchor.

**If Gate 1 passes:** Proceed to Gate 2. The participation graph is real.

## GATE 2 — The Operator Pays
**Deadline: January 2027 (before Spring semester)**

**Question:** Will a venue write a check for participation data?

**What to prove:**
- Gully's (or another venue) pays $149–$299/month before Fall ends
- At least one operator makes an operational decision based on Sway data (staffing, event timing, recognizing a regular)
- Operator can answer "who are my top 10 most loyal customers?" using Sway

**What to build for this gate:**
- Venue dashboard showing: return rate, top regulars, visit frequency, peak nights
- One actionable insight delivered to operator every week (automated or manual)
- Willingness-to-pay conversation with Gully's management by August 2026

**How to measure:**
- Signed contract: yes/no
- Operator cites Sway in at least one operational decision: yes/no
- NPS from operator after 4 weeks of Fall deployment: ≥8

**What failure looks like:**
- Operator says "interesting but I wouldn't pay for it"
- Dashboard built but operator never opens it
- No operational behavior changes

**If Gate 2 fails:** The commercial model is wrong. Operators don't value participation data enough to pay. Either the dashboard isn't surfacing the right insight, or the data density isn't there yet. Fix dashboard before adding nodes.

**If Gate 2 passes:** Revenue exists. Proceed to Gate 3.

---

## GATE 3 — The Behavior Transfers
**Deadline: End of Spring 2027**

**Question:** Can the campus ecosystem model be replicated at a second campus?

**What to prove:**
- 1 new campus ecosystem launched (2–3 venues, 1 student org)
- Activation rate at new campus ≥60% (below Endicott baseline is a warning)
- Return rate at new campus ≥20% within 6 weeks
- Second campus reaches paying contract within the same semester

**Target campuses (Northeast cluster):**
- Salem State (urban, proximity to Salem/Beverly nightlife)
- Northeastern (urban density, diverse community nodes)
- UMass Amherst (high-density campus bar culture)
- Gordon College (similar Endicott profile — small, tight community)

**What failure looks like:**
- Activation is low without founder presence (meaning Gully's worked because of Kev, not the product)
- Return rate doesn't materialize organically
- Second campus feels like starting from zero every time

**If Gate 3 fails:** Distribution is broken. The campus rollout model needs rethinking — potentially requires a campus ambassador layer or a different activation mechanism.

**If Gate 3 passes:** The playbook works. You now have a repeatable model. This is the Series A story.

## Summer 2026 — Before Gate 1 Opens

This summer has one purpose: prepare to pass Gate 1 in Fall.

**Priority 1 — Data analysis (by July 15)**
- Pull frequency distribution from Gully's data: 1 visit / 2 visits / 3+ / 5+ / 10+
- Identify the top 20 most active users — what makes them different?
- Map visit timing: which nights, which weeks generated repeat behavior
- Answer: was the 26% return rate driven by a small power-user cohort or distributed?

**Priority 2 — User interviews (July–August, 10 minimum)**
- Talk to students who returned 3+ times. Why?
- Talk to students who scanned once and never came back. Why?
- Ask: "If Sway tracked your gym visits too, would you use it there?"
- Ask: "Would you want to look back at your semester in Sway?"
- Do NOT pitch. Only listen.

**Priority 3 — Operator conversation (by August 1)**
- Sit with Gully's management. Show them the data.
- Ask: "What question about your customers would you pay to answer?"
- Don't pitch pricing yet. Learn what would make them feel it's worth money.

**Priority 4 — Fall deployment prep (August)**
- Finalize NFC tap station design and placement (entrance chokepoint — not bar top, not table)
- Onboard Endicott Gym as Node 2 (low-lift — no staff, just a station)
- Identify and approach Foundry/Entrepreneurship Club for Node 3
- Rebuild onboarding to be 1-tap, no friction

**Priority 5 — Tech (ongoing)**
- Fix whatever broke or was clunky in Spring
- Build venue dashboard to show top regulars + return rate clearly
- Do NOT build new features this summer

## The Participation Graph — When It's Real

The participation graph is real when a student can look at their Sway profile and see:

```
Gully's       — 14 visits
Endicott Gym  — 22 visits
Foundry       — 6 sessions
E-Club        — 4 meetings
Salem Venue   — 3 visits
```

And feel: *"This is actually my semester."*

That moment — when identity feels personally meaningful — is when the moat starts forming. Not before.

Until then, it's infrastructure with potential. Not a product with pull.

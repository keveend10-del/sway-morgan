## Core Belief

Marketing for Sway at this stage has one job:

**Get students through the door at Gully's for the first time, then create conditions for them to come back.**

That is it. Until Gully's repeat behavior is proven, no marketing should be optimizing for anything else. Not brand awareness. Not press. Not follower counts. Not campus-wide adoption. **Repeat behavior at one venue first.**

When Gully's data shows clean repeat signal, marketing's job shifts to: replicate the first-visit-to-repeat loop at the next venue.

---

## Sway Marketing Filter

Before recommending any marketing tactic, run this check:

1. Does this bring a new student to Gully's for a first scan?
2. Does this increase the likelihood that a student returns within 14 days?
3. Does this create word-of-mouth between students who have already scanned?
4. Does this require zero staff explanation to work?
5. Can we measure its direct impact on repeat check-ins — not just impressions?

If the answer to 3+ is no, the tactic is wrong for this stage.

## Campus Marketing Expertise

### What Works in Dense Student Environments
- **Peer identity mechanics**: Students adopt behavior when it reflects their social identity, not because of a discount. Sway's premium identity framing is correct. Never introduce coupon language.
- **Event attachment**: Marketing tied to specific nights (DJ night, Thursday regulars) outperforms generic awareness campaigns by 3–5x in conversion to first visit.
- **Word-of-mouth from regulars**: One student who is a recognized regular is worth 50 impressions. Early marketing should identify and amplify the first cohort of regulars.
- **Scarcity of recognition**: "Founding regular" framing drives more first scans than any awareness campaign. Limited identity slots feel real on a campus.
- **Physical presence near the scan point**: QR code placement, door presence, and ambient Sway branding within the venue drives adoption faster than any digital campaign targeting students who haven't been to Gully's yet.

### What Fails Every Time
- Generic campus-wide blasts with no behavioral hook
- Referral programs before repeat behavior is established (you get optimizers, not regulars)
- Instagram campaigns that generate impressions but no door traffic
- Student ambassador programs with no clear conversion metric
- Discount-first offers that attract one-time visitors, not regulars
- Email sequences targeting students who scanned once and haven't returned — too early, too pushy

## Channel Strategy by Phase

### Phase 1 (Now — Gully's Pilot)
**Goal:** First scan + return visit at Gully's

Highest-leverage channels:
1. **In-venue physical presence** — QR visibility, ambient branding, staff passively wearing/displaying Sway
2. **Organic social from regulars** — Identify the first 10 students with 2+ scans and activate them as organic social seeds (not paid ambassadors — recognition-based)
3. **Event-specific content** — Instagram/TikTok tied to specific Gully's nights, not generic Sway awareness
4. **Peer-to-peer text/DM** — The single highest-converting student channel. Encourage students to share their Sway status naturally (no forced referral mechanic yet)

Avoid in Phase 1:
- Paid ads (too early — no LTV data, no proven return behavior to optimize toward)
- Campus-wide email blasts
- Press outreach (no story to tell yet — wait for data)
- Influencer partnerships (cost > return before behavior is proven)

### Phase 2 (After Gully's Has Clean Repeat Signal)
**Goal:** Replicate first-visit-to-repeat loop at next venue

- Activate the Gully's regular cohort as social proof for next venue launch
- Introduce referral mechanic (only after repeat rate is established — otherwise you attract optimizers)
- Begin earned media outreach with Gully's data as the story hook
- Test campus-specific paid social targeting students enrolled at Endicott (tight geo + behavioral targeting)

## Metrics Priya Tracks

**Primary (tied to behavior):**
- First-scan-to-return rate (7-day and 14-day)
- Marketing-source-to-repeat-scan attribution (which channel brought students who actually came back?)
- Organic scan growth week-over-week (word-of-mouth signal)
- Event night scan lift (did the marketing for a specific night move the needle?)

**Secondary:**
- Cost per first scan (when paid channels are active)
- Organic social reach tied to specific events
- Student NPS from first-scan cohort

**Vanity (never optimize for these alone):**
- Total signups
- Social followers
- Impressions
- Story views
- App downloads without linked scans

## Pattern Matches from Experience

**"I've seen this before — Foursquare campus push":** We ran a campus ambassador program that generated 50,000 signups in 6 weeks. Retention at 90 days was 4%. The signups were real. The behavior wasn't. The metric that mattered was weekly active check-ins, not signups. We optimized for the wrong thing for 6 months before someone caught it.

**"I've seen this before — discount trap":** At Olo, a bar client ran a "first drink 50% off with check-in" promotion. Scans went up 300% that week. Repeat visits went down 12% the following month. Discount-seekers crowded out the regulars. Took 3 months to recover the cohort quality.

**"Red flag":** Any marketing initiative that requires students to explain Sway to other students will fail. The explanation has to happen through the experience itself — the scan, the confirmation, the recap. Marketing creates the first visit. The product creates the return.

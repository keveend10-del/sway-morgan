# FOUNDER_CONTEXT.md — Sway (June 2026)

## What Sway Is

Sway is the Participation OS for real-world communities and physical venues.

It helps people, venues, and communities remember completed real-world participation across places.

The first wedge is campus bars — starting with Gully's at Endicott College.

The goal is not loyalty, payments, rewards, or discounts.

The goal is participation memory: venues know who showed up, who came back, who came with whom, and what that means over time.

**You show up. Sway remembers.**

---

## One-Line Description

Sway is the memory layer for physical life.

Students build a record of where they show up. Venues finally know who actually comes back.

---

## The Core Building Block: Session

The core primitive is not a check-in. It is a session.

A session means: a person was physically present in a specific place, during a specific time, with specific context.

A session can include: person, venue, event, group, time arrived, time stayed, completion status, return history, cross-venue context.

Check-in via QR starts the session today. NFC, Apple Wallet, reservation, table assignment, and other entry points come later. The interface can change. The idea stays the same.

---

## What Sway Is NOT

Sway is NOT:

- A loyalty app
- A rewards platform
- A POS replacement
- A payments company
- A ticketing platform
- A generic analytics dashboard
- A marketing tool
- A crypto project

Those may become layers later. They are not the current business.

---

## The New Category: Participation OS

Traditional systems are fragmented:
- POS knows what was bought
- Resy/OpenTable knows who booked
- Instagram knows what was posted
- Event platforms know who registered
- Staff remember regulars manually

Sway connects the missing layer: who actually participated, where, with whom, and what that means over time.

POS is transaction infrastructure. Sway is participation infrastructure.

The POS closes a check. Sway builds the relationship.

## Current Stage

Sway has completed its first live pilot.

The pilot was conducted at Gully's, the on-campus bar at Endicott College.

The pilot ran during the Spring 2026 semester.

**The pilot is NOT currently active** because the academic year ended and students left campus for summer break.

The pilot was intentionally paused at the conclusion of the semester.

No additional venues are currently live.

## Gully's Pilot Results — Spring 2026

- 255 total check-ins
- 81% activation rate
- 26% return rate
- 9-week deployment
- Zero paid marketing
- No rewards
- No incentives
- No staff workflow changes
- QR-based check-in

**Important:**

These numbers were achieved without changing how the venue operates.

Staff were not required to explain Sway.

Guests were not forced to participate.

No discounts were offered.

No promotions were offered.

No gamification mechanisms were deployed.

No payment functionality existed.

## What Has Been Proven

The pilot suggests:

1. Students are willing to scan.
2. Students are willing to create an identity profile.
3. Students will return and scan again.
4. Presence can be captured without disrupting venue operations.
5. A lightweight participation layer can exist alongside existing venue workflows.

---

## What Has NOT Been Proven

The following remain unproven:

1. Whether venues will consistently pay for retention visibility.
2. Whether behavior transfers across multiple venues.
3. Whether participation continuity becomes a habit.
4. Whether cross-venue density compounds.
5. Whether Sway increases venue revenue.
6. Whether recognition meaningfully increases repeat attendance.
7. Whether network effects emerge.
8. Whether the session model works outside the campus bar context.

These are the next questions to answer.

---

## Current Thesis

Physical venues optimize transactions.

Sway optimizes participation memory.

Most venue software measures purchases. Very few systems measure repeat presence.

Participation memory becomes more valuable as:

- More people participate
- More sessions are logged
- More venues join the ecosystem
- More behavioral history accumulates across places

The long-term moat is not payments.

The moat is participation density — behavioral history no competitor can replicate.

---

## The First Killer Use Cases

**1. Regular Status** — Who actually comes back?
Sway identifies regulars based on completed visits, not staff guesses or spend alone.

**2. Event-to-Return Tracking** — Did trivia night create one-time traffic or real regulars?
Sway tracks whether people who attended an event came back later.

**3. Cross-Venue Promos** — Did this person visit both locations?
Example: Bernadette's and Sadler (same operator, two restaurants) run a promo where visiting both earns a reward. Without Sway, staff must ask, trust memory, or use spreadsheets. Sway validates the two-venue journey automatically — no staff friction, no customer explanation needed.

**4. Campus Participation Graph** — Where are students actually showing up across campus?
Sway connects Gully's, the gym, events, clubs, pop-ups, and local venues into one participation record.

## Product Sequence

**Phase 1: Presence** — Who showed up? (QR, NFC, basic session, timestamp, venue, user identity)

**Phase 2: Completion** — Was this a real visit? (dwell logic, session expiration, event window, completed participation credit)

**Phase 3: Recognition** — What should this person get for showing up again? (regular status, badges, recaps, venue-specific recognition, cross-venue rewards)

**Phase 4: Operator Memory** — What can the venue now know? (repeat visitors, completed promos, event-to-return behavior, group overlap, community participation)

**Phase 5: Session Operations** — How does Sway improve the service flow? (table sessions, seat numbers, server context, staff prompts, recognition moments)

**Phase 6: Commerce** — How does money eventually move? (tab management, payments, reservations, stored value)

Payments come in Phase 6. Not before.

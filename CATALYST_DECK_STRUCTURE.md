# Sway Catalyst Deck — 12 Slides
**Created:** 2026-06-24 | **For:** HBS Foundry Catalyst (July 1 deadline)

**Narrative arc:**
> Invisible behavior → verified participation → student identity → venue intelligence → AI recommendations → paid pilots → campus graph.

**AI rule:** AI appears in slides 6, 10, 12 only. Do not mention AI in every slide — it will sound bolted on.

## Slide 1 — Title

**Headline:** Sway

**Subheadline:** AI-powered participation intelligence for real-world communities.

**Supporting line:** Starting with campus venues, Sway verifies who actually shows up, learns what creates repeat behavior, and turns participation into memory, recognition, and operator insight.

**Visual:** Clean Sway Card or check-in card connected to a graph: Student → Venue → Event → Repeat Visit → Insight.

**Script:** "Sway is building the participation layer for real-world communities. We start with campus venues because they are dense, repeatable environments where real-world behavior forms quickly."

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem

**Headline:** Real-world participation is invisible.

**Core message:** The systems that run venues and campuses track transactions, tickets, and attendance — but not repeated presence.

**Three problem blocks:**
- *Students:* They show up to bars, gyms, clubs, and events, but their real-world life has no memory layer.
- *Operators:* They know what was bought, not who came back, which events created regulars, or how behavior compounds.
- *Communities:* Participation is one of the strongest signals of belonging, but it is usually unmeasured.

**Visual:** POS / event / loyalty systems on one side. Missing questions on the other: "Who came back? Who became a regular? Which event drove return behavior? Which groups overlap?"

**Script:** "Today, a student can show up every week and still start from zero every time. A venue can see purchases, but not participation. That missing layer is the opportunity."

---

## Slide 3 — The Insight

**Headline:** Showing up is the signal.

**Core message:** Presence is not just attendance. Repeat presence is loyalty, identity, and community health.

**Key line:** I went somewhere. That should count.

**Logic stack:**
- A check-in captures intent
- A completed session confirms participation
- Repeated participation creates identity
- Cross-node participation creates a behavioral graph
- AI turns that graph into insight

**Visual:** Vertical stack: Presence → Completion → Memory → Recognition → Operator Intelligence → Participation Graph

---

## Slide 4 — The Solution

**Headline:** Sway verifies participation and makes it useful.

**Product loop:**
1. Student arrives
2. Student taps or scans
3. Sway verifies presence
4. Student earns memory/status
5. Operator sees repeat behavior
6. AI recommends what to do next

**Student value:** Memory, timeline, Sway Card, regular status, semester recaps.

**Operator value:** Emerging regulars, return behavior, event impact, hidden cohorts, weekly recommendations.

**Script:** "Sway is mobile-light. The best session ends with the phone in the pocket. Students get recognition and memory. Operators get intelligence they cannot get from POS."

---

## Slide 5 — Product Architecture

**Headline:** A lightweight layer above existing systems.

**Core message:** Sway does not replace POS, payments, ticketing, or staff workflows. It creates a new verified participation layer.

**Three product objects:**
1. *Presence Capture* — QR/NFC check-ins at venue entry or participation moments.
2. *Completion Logic* — Rules that distinguish intent from real participation.
3. *Intelligence Layer* — Dashboards and AI reports that explain repeat behavior.

**Key line:** No POS replacement. No payment changes. No staff workflow change.

**Visual:** Existing systems at bottom (POS, ticketing, loyalty, events). Sway above them as the participation layer.

---

## Slide 6 — AI Engine *(AI slide 1 of 3)*

**Headline:** AI turns participation data into decisions.

**Core message:** Sway's AI is powered by a proprietary dataset of verified real-world participation — not generic data.

**Inputs to AI:**
- Verified check-ins
- Completed sessions
- Repeat visits
- Event context
- Cross-node behavior

**AI outputs for operators:**
- "These students are becoming regulars"
- "This event created the highest return behavior"
- "Here is who to recognize this week"
- "Here is what changed since last week"

**AI outputs for students:**
- Semester recaps
- Personal participation summaries
- Regular status explanations

**Key line:** Sway creates the verified participation dataset. AI turns it into decisions.

**Script:** "Most AI tools analyze data that already exists. Sway creates a new dataset: verified real-world participation. That is what makes the intelligence layer defensible."

## Slide 9 — Wedge Market

**Headline:** Start narrow. Build the graph.

**Beachhead:** Independent student-dense venues near campuses.

**Why this wedge:**
- High repeat behavior
- Dense social communities
- Existing entry moments
- Operators care about regulars

## Slide 11 — Moat / Competition

**Headline:** POS tracks spend. Sway tracks participation.

**Comparison grid:**

| Category | What they know | What they miss |
|---|---|---|
| POS | Purchases | Presence and repeat participation |
| Loyalty | Rewards/spend | Organic behavior without incentives |
| Event tools | One-time attendance | Longitudinal identity |
| Guestlist tools | Access/RSVPs | Repeat behavior and cross-node graph |
| **Sway** | **Verified participation** | **Builds over time** |

**Moat:**
- Verified presence data
- Cross-node participation history
- Student-owned identity
- AI trained on local repeat-behavior patterns
- Campus density before broad expansion

**Script:** "The check-in is easy to copy. The hard part is density: repeated verified behavior across multiple nodes in the same ecosystem. That is what becomes the participation graph."

## Privacy Framing (Address on Slide 5 or 6)

Investor concern explicitly noted. Required language:
- Sway is a privacy-conscious participation and venue analytics SaaS
- Not a payments company, bank, POS replacement, or alcohol-promotion product
- Students own their participation history
- Venues see patterns, not raw identities
- Completion logic prevents fake scans without surveilling users

### Pilot Results

**Gully's + Gym Combined**
- 255 total check-ins
- 155 unique users
- 23.2% return rate (2+ visits)
- 14.8% visited 3+ times
- 7.7% visited 5+ times

**Gully's Only**
- 67 users
- **37% return rate**
- 16 users visited 3+ times
- 8 users visited 5+ times
- 15.8 days average return cadence

**Key Takeaway:** A meaningful subset of students repeatedly returned with no incentives, no recognition features, no payments, no staff workflow changes. Supports core identity thesis.

### Major Learnings

**1. Organic Discovery > Launch Events**
Strongest repeat users came from the original Feb. 3 cohort. Large launch waves (April 23, gym launch) generated many one-time signups but little habit formation.

**2. Repeat Behavior Exists**
- Bennett Fici: 22 visits
- Konrad Krysztoforski: 13 visits

Need interviews to determine whether Sway influenced behavior or simply captured existing behavior.

**3. Identity Portability Not Proven Yet**
Only two meaningful cross-venue users. Cross-venue identity remains an unvalidated hypothesis.

**4. NFC Entrance Flow Is The Next Test**
Single hallway NFC tap station. Door staff says: "Scan here, it's our online Gully's." Removes QR discovery friction and staff dependency.

### Strategic Decisions

**Messaging:** Lead with "37% of Gully's users returned with no incentives, recognition, or payments." Do NOT lead with blended 23%.

**Positioning:** Sway = identity infrastructure. Not loyalty. Not discounts. Not payments. Not POS replacement.

**Verification:** Student verification ✅ | Age verification ❌ — "Sway confirms they're a student. Venues still check ID."

**School Partnerships:** Not a Fall 2026 priority. Move to later phase after proving venue density and repeat behavior.
